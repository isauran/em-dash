About the Em Dash

The em dash is named after its width—traditionally, it's as wide as the capital letter M. It's longer than both the hyphen (-) and the en dash (–). Em dashes can replace commas, semicolons, colons, and parentheses to indicate added emphasis, interruption, or an abrupt change of thought.

Remember—despite what the internet says—not only AI writers love using em dashes excessively. Human writers have been overusing them—quite masterfully—for centuries!
